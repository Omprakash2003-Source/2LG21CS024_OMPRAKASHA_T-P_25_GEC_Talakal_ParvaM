History

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Vijayanagara district of Karnataka, India.It's known for its grand palaces, temples, and waterworks, and was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire from 1336 to 1565. Hampi was a prosperous city with many temples, farms, and markets. The city was conquered by the Deccan Muslim confederacy in 1565 and pillaged for six months before being abandoned. The ruins of Hampi have been a site of archaeological study since the 19th century.
